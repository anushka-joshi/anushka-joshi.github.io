Course Overview

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to deep learning — from the mathematical foundations to modern architectures deployed at scale. Students will build intuition through theory, implement models from scratch, and conduct rigorous experimental analysis.

Intended to give foundational and hands-on knowledge of:

  • Deep learning methods — neural networks, backpropagation, optimisation, convolutional networks, recurrent models, transformers, and generative approaches.
  • A strong practical understanding of foundational concepts of deep learning, including loss landscapes, regularisation strategies, and the art of training stable models.
  • Experimental analysis — designing ablation studies, reading and reproducing papers, evaluating results with statistical rigour, and communicating findings clearly.

Prerequisites

Linear algebra, probability, and multivariable calculus at an undergraduate level. Proficiency in Python. Prior exposure to machine learning.
